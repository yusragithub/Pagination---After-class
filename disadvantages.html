Disadvantages

Practical Limitations:

  1. Limited Range:
    2. Typically 200-300 miles, requiring frequent recharging.
  2. Charging Time:
    3. Can take 30 minutes to several hours to fully charge.
  3. Charging Infrastructure: Limited availability, particularly in rural areas.
  4. Higher Upfront Cost:
    5. EVs are often pricier than gasoline-powered vehicles.

Technical Limitations:

  1. Battery Durability:
    2. Battery degradation over time reduces range and overall performance.
  2. Limited Standardization:
    3. Different charging systems and connectors.
  3. Electrical Grid Strain:
    4. Increased demand on electrical grids.

Environmental Concerns:

  1. Resource Extraction:
    2. Mining of lithium, cobalt, and other materials for batteries.
  2. Battery Disposal:
    3. Difficulties in recycling and disposing of spent batteries.
  3. Production Emissions:
    4. EV production generates significant greenhouse gas emissions.
