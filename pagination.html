Electric Vehicles (EVs)

Electric Vehicles (EVs) are environmentally friendly transportation solutions that utilize electric motors powered by electricity stored in rechargeable batteries, replacing traditional internal combustion engines that burn fossil fuels.

Key Components:

Electric Motor:
Converts electrical energy into mechanical energy. Battery Pack: Stores energy, typically using lithium-ion cells.
Power Electronics Controller:
Manages energy flow between motor and battery.
Charging System:
Replenishes battery from external power sources. On-Board Charger: Converts AC power to DC for charging.
Key Characteristics:
Electric motor propulsion Rechargeable battery storage No internal combustion engine Zero tailpipe emissions Regenerative braking
How EVs Work:
Electricity from the battery powers the electric motor.The motor converts electrical energy into mechanical energy.
